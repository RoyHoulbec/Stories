Brace Yourself

Brace yourself
For the realization,
To smack you across
The face and
Leave a cold lesson imprinted
On your cheek.
It says,
This is not a story arc,
This is not a parable,
There’s no moral here,
Fuck you.










Lake Superior

Faraway faces
And miscounted graces,
Keep my feet
In time with my paces.
As I walk from well lit places,
Through rain spattered streets,
Past terminal cases.

I want to wring the night, from the clouds,
into a cup and drink from it.
Not for pleasure, but the satisfaction of an acquired taste.
Alieving not which I know nor hate,
but with a tried indifference accept.

A receipt for something you did not want,
Toss me as you can toss other things.
So I matter no longer.
So I am no longer required to be something which I don't know what it is.










Cacophony

Now seen clear,
it wafts and sways as if it will fall,
This crystal chandelier.
Its reflections are my thoughts.
and when it crashes upon the floor,
The innumerable pieces
will gleam brighter than ever before.










Shelfed

The library is vast.
Its shelves reach in every direction.
The books are many and their pages innumerable,
but here at an obscure hour on an obscure page
someone is reading these words.










Sarcophagi

Where lie the hopes of great men.
Amid pangs that echo in barrow halls,
Softly chimes the requiem of our potential.
To it still dusts plays out the aimless waltz,
Each to no purpose, to no affliction, to no avail.

Ahab

Need it stand?
Need it stand?
for head in heart and heart in hand?
Need the eucharists of the past,
tip its hat to your deeds?
No, I will not pedal in soft spoken pangs,
Nor will I sigh by the river tossing pebbles among the reeds.
I will shun the biddings of the quiet universe,
I will abdicate in feverous silence the sins of the sun and of the stars,
and by the light of a candle pass their punishment on a soft spoken page,
In a single phrase,
I will expunge the fallacies of my breeding.
In a single word,
I will inherit everything that was never promised to me.










The Great White Buffalo

I have seen the white buffalo
and now I know it roams these hills,
forsaken as I am.
I search,
I see it nought,
except in my sleep.
wandering in mysterious rhythms.
keeping just out of reach.










Wake Up

Inside
The day is all a buzz and hum
And all a daze and all a drum
And adverbs pile up
like a list of things to be done
like a pile of leaves baking in the sun
On a not so autumn day.

Go look, go touch, go feel, it's bright outside!
but you're pursuing your pillow
For the comfiest spot
With your alarm snoozing
Ruminating on plate
Served up to sleep
But breakfast is sacred
And the window is open
And The shrubbery looks nice
In the morning.

Outside
But where are the people, where are the places?
I hear voices, I see faces?
They are everywhere,
following other currents of life, of which I am not a part, swimming past,
into a river of bodies that somewhere must lead
to a sea where there are many fish.

Night
Day is cacophony of light and sound.
The night is punctuated.
The night is for nouns.

Festooned faces
fragile frowns
Quite swimming simple sounds
Shimmering places with simple bounds

Talk, laugh,
Pray, listen.
Do you hear you heart rate quicken?
At the sound of nostalgic steps.

will you turn?
And turn alast,
To the look that looks not through or past.
A moment, a smile, put in a cast,

Because it is inherently broken.

Will you see the present as it presented?

No, I am not forthcoming, I am not attentive
the world is in many pieces
And I don't know how they go together










Language Barriers

My guitar won't play what I tell it to.
I’ve tried to learn some of its language
But it won’t learn any of mine
It makes my words feel clumsy and foolish
I have seen it talk to other people
and they have beautiful conversations.
